Hermosillo, Sonora

The Hermosillo Cathedral, also called La Catedral de la Asunción, is one of the most visited places in the city. Its architectural style could be classified as baroque, neoclassical and neo-Gothic. It is 30 meters tall.

Sonora has a reputation for producing fine cuts of beef like Angus, Herefords and Holsteins.

Parque La Ruina seeks to offer the community a pleasant space for coexistence where they can find a wide variety of street food of the highest quality, enjoy great events and spend days and nights enjoying the best music.

The Museo de Arte de Sonora is a space for the dissemination and promotion of art and culture that seeks to strengthen people, providing significant experiences that help them improve their relationship with the environment.

The beach is named in honor of Eusebio Francisco Kino, who visited the site during his mission work in the 17th century. An indigenous people called the Seri had lived here long before that. A group of fishermen landed on the beach in 1930 and founded what is now known as Kino Viejo.
